# The Five Pillars of Sovereignty — Defined

*Concrete criteria for the Bruce Wayne sovereignty system.*

---

## Pillar 1: Identity Sovereignty

**Good enough definition:** You operate through multiple personas with clear separation, and your true self never touches the public chain.

### Criteria

| # | Criterion | Audit Question |
|---|-----------|----------------|
| 1.1 | **Multiple personas exist** | Do I have at least 3 distinct operational identities? |
| 1.2 | **Personas are separated** | Can an observer of Persona A deduce Persona B's existence? |
| 1.3 | **True self is air-gapped** | Does my public-facing identity have any link to my legal identity? |
| 1.4 | **Privacy tools are default** | Do I use privacy-preserving infrastructure for >80% of operations? |
| 1.5 | **Batcave identity is maintained** | Do I have one identity that has never touched a public blockchain? |

### Scoring Rubric

- **5/5 (Sovereign):** All criteria met, personas fully isolated, regular audits clean
- **4/5 (Strong):** 4 criteria met, minor leakage between 1-2 personas
- **3/5 (Developing):** 3 criteria met, significant exposure risk
- **2/5 (Vulnerable):** 2 criteria met, single point of failure in identity
- **1/5 (Exposed):** 1 or fewer criteria met, full public exposure

## Pillar 2: Capital Sovereignty

**Good enough definition:** Your capital structure is a fortress—diversified, resilient, and generates ongoing revenue independent of market conditions.

### Criteria

| # | Criterion | Audit Question |
|---|-----------|----------------|
| 2.1 | **Diversified holdings** | Is my capital spread across ≥3 asset classes (crypto, equity, RWA)? |
| 2.2 | **Escape velocity maintained** | Do I have 6+ months of expenses in BTC/ETH/stablecoins in cold storage? |
| 2.3 | **Zero recourse debt** | Am I borrowing only through overcollateralized positions? |
| 2.4 | **Toll generation active** | Do I have ≥2 revenue streams that generate fees/basis points? |
| 2.5 | **Protocol risk distributed** | Is no single protocol holding >20% of my liquid capital? |

### Scoring Rubric

- **5/5 (Sovereign):** All criteria met, multiple toll streams, fortress capital
- **4/5 (Strong):** 4 criteria met, solid diversification, minor concentration
- **3/5 (Developing):** 3 criteria met, some fragility in structure
- **2/5 (Vulnerable):** 2 criteria met, concentrated exposure, debt risk
- **1/5 (Exposed):** 1 or fewer criteria met, single points of failure throughout

## Pillar 3: Information Sovereignty

**Good enough definition:** You see what others cannot, position before moves, and your own activity remains strategically opaque.

### Criteria

| # | Criterion | Audit Question |
|---|-----------|----------------|
| 3.1 | **Monitoring systems active** | Do I have automated alerts for whale moves, governance proposals, dev activity? |
| 3.2 | **Pattern recognition trained** | Can I identify 3+ "invisible" signals before they're public? |
| 3.3 | **Strategic opacity maintained** | Do I reveal positions only after they're no longer actionable? |
| 3.4 | **Information network exists** | Do I have ≥3 relationships that share pre-public insights? |
| 3.5 | **Edge is documented** | Do I maintain a log of predictions vs. outcomes to validate edge? |

### Scoring Rubric

- **5/5 (Sovereign):** All criteria met, systematic edge, network provides alpha
- **4/5 (Strong):** 4 criteria met, good monitoring, some opacity gaps
- **3/5 (Developing):** 3 criteria met, reactive rather than predictive
- **2/5 (Vulnerable):** 2 criteria met, information parity with market
- **1/5 (Exposed):** 1 or fewer criteria met, constantly surprised by moves

## Pillar 4: Relational Sovereignty

**Good enough definition:** You have people, not just contracts—and they would shelter you if everything collapsed.

### Criteria

| # | Criterion | Audit Question |
|---|-----------|----------------|
| 4.1 | **First circle defined** | Do I have 3-5 people who know my full strategy? |
| 4.2 | **Contributions without expectation** | Have I helped ≥5 people in the last 30 days without asking for return? |
| 4.3 | **Partnerships over contracts** | Do my key relationships include upside sharing, not just fee splits? |
| 4.4 | **Bridge maintenance** | Have I reached out to my first circle in the last 7 days? |
| 4.5 | **Reputation capital** | Would 3+ people vouch for me publicly if I needed shelter? |

### Scoring Rubric

- **5/5 (Sovereign):** All criteria met, unbreakable first circle, network as immune system
- **4/5 (Strong):** 4 criteria met, solid relationships, minor neglect
- **3/5 (Developing):** 3 criteria met, transactional rather than relational
- **2/5 (Vulnerable):** 2 criteria met, isolated, few genuine allies
- **1/5 (Exposed):** 1 or fewer criteria met, solo operator, no safety net

---

## Pillar 5: Legacy Sovereignty

**Good enough definition:** What you built runs without you—and you've begun letting go.

### Criteria

| # | Criterion | Audit Question |
|---|-----------|----------------|
| 5.1 | **Systems documented** | Is critical infrastructure documented so others can operate it? |
| 5.2 | **Decentralization in progress** | Do my protocols have governance or community ownership mechanisms? |
| 5.3 | **Successor identified** | Have I identified ≥1 person who could carry the mission forward? |
| 5.4 | **Knowledge shared** | Have I mentored or taught someone in the last 30 days? |
| 5.5 | **Institution building** | Is there a culture/purpose beyond just my personal involvement? |

### Scoring Rubric

- **5/5 (Sovereign):** All criteria met, systems autonomous, actively letting go
- **4/5 (Strong):** 4 criteria met, good documentation, succession in planning
- **3/5 (Developing):** 3 criteria met, some decentralization, still central
- **2/5 (Vulnerable):** 2 criteria met, everything depends on you
- **1/5 (Exposed):** 1 or fewer criteria met, no continuity plan, dies with you
