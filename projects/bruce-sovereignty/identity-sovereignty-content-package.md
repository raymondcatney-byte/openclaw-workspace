**Tweet 6/10:**
Layer 4: Governance

Protocol participation, DAO voting, forum discussions.

Connected to governance tokens, not identity or capital. Strategic voting alignment.

Different communication patterns. Separate wallet per protocol.

**Tweet 7/10:**
Layer 5: The Sandbox

Testing new protocols, experimenting, gathering intelligence.

Completely isolated. Burner wallets. Assumed compromise.

Minimal capital at risk. Burn regularly.

**Tweet 8/10:**
The Separation Checklist:

✓ Device separation (different hardware)
✓ Network separation (different VPNs/IPs)
✓ Temporal separation (different activity hours)
✓ Communication separation (different emails/phones)
✓ Financial separation (no overlapping wallets)
✓ Social separation (no overlapping followers)

**Tweet 9/10:**

Using the same wallet across layers = instant linking via blockchain analysis

Prevention:
→ New address for every transaction
→ Separate wallets for each layer
→ Privacy-preserving protocols when bridging

**Tweet 10/10:**
Identity sovereignty isn't about having something to hide.

It's about having something to protect.

Your strategies. Your positions. Your optionality.

Build your layers.

## THREAD 2: The 7 Identity Failures

**Tweet 1/8:**
7 ways your identity separation is failing (and you don't know it):

I learned these the hard way. 🧵

**Tweet 2/8:**
1. The Address Reuse

Same wallet across layers = instant linking

Fix: New address for every transaction. Separate wallets per layer.

**Tweet 3/8:**
2. The Twitter Link

Accidentally posting your trading wallet or revealing connections

Fix: Never post addresses. If you must share, use fresh addresses with no history.

**Tweet 4/8:**
3. The Timezone Leak

Activity patterns across layers reveal single operator

Fix: Schedule posts randomly. Trade irregular hours. Use VPNs in different timezones.

**Tweet 5/8:**
4. The Linguistic Fingerprint

Writing style analysis links your pseudonymous accounts

Fix: Deliberately vary style. Different vocabulary. Minimal text for capital layers.

**Tweet 6/8:**
5. The Device Contamination

Malware spreads from test layer to capital layer

Fix: Physical device separation. Regular wipes. No shared USBs.

**Tweet 7/8:**
6. The Social Graph Leak

Someone who knows you connects dots across layers

Fix: Compartmentalize who knows what. No one person knows all layers.

**Tweet 8/8:**
7. The On-Ramp Trace

KYC exchange links your fiat identity to crypto

Fix: Layer 3 never touches KYC. Use DEXs, P2P, privacy-preserving on-ramps.

Audit your setup. Most people have 3+ of these failures.
