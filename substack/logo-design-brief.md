# Logo Design Brief: The Architect's Dispatch

## Concept Direction

**Core Idea:** Architectural precision meets shadowy intelligence

**Visual Metaphor:** A blueprint that only reveals its full structure to those who look closely. Hidden in plain sight.

## Design Elements

### Primary Symbol Options

**Option 1: The Compass-Blueprint**
- Stylized compass rose
- Constructed from blueprint lines (thin, technical)
- One quadrant "in shadow" or "under construction"
- Represents: Navigation through incomplete information

**Option 2: The Arch**
- Gothic or brutalist arch form
- Negative space creates subtle "A" for Architect
- Keystone is distinct (different color or shape)
- Represents: Structure, entry point, foundation

**Option 3: The Grid/Coordinates**
- Minimalist grid pattern
- One point highlighted (the "signal")
- Subtle perspective distortion (3D depth)
- Represents: Precision, location, finding the real

### Color Palette

**Primary:** 
- Deep charcoal/near-black: `#0a0a0a` (sophistication, depth)
- Amber/gold accent: `#c9a227` or `#ff9f00` (intelligence, warmth)

**Secondary:**
- Steel gray: `#2a2a2a` (structure, industrial)
- Off-white/cream: `#f5f5f0` (parchment, archives)

**Usage:**
- Dark backgrounds for digital (Substack, Twitter)
- Light backgrounds for print (journals, letters)

### Typography Lockup

**"THE ARCHITECT'S"**
- Font: Thin, all-caps, wide tracking
- Weight: Light (200-300)
- Size: Smaller than main word

**"DISPATCH"**
- Font: Bold, distinctive
- Weight: Heavy (700-900)
- Style: Slightly condensed, strong presence

**Font Pairings:**
- Primary: Bebas Neue (impact, architectural)
- Alternative: Monument Extended (luxury, editorial)
- Fallback: Inter Bold (clean, technical)

### Specifications

**Digital (Substack header, Twitter avatar):**
- Avatar: 400x400px, circular crop
- Header: 1500x500px, landscape
- Format: PNG (transparent background), SVG (scalable)

**Print (Journal covers, wax seals):**
- Minimum 300 DPI
- Black and white version (for embossing)
- Single-color version (for wax seals, stamps)

## Mood References

**Visual Influences:**
- Deus Ex: Human Revolution UI (gold/amber on black)
- Mies van der Rohe blueprints (precision, minimalism)
- Deus Ex: Mankind Divided architecture (brutalist, imposing)
- The Batman (2022) title cards (gothic, modern)
- Kinfolk magazine (editorial, restrained)

**Emotional Tone:**
- Confident but not arrogant
- Sophisticated but not elitist
- Technical but not cold
- Shadowy but not ominous

## What to Avoid

❌ Gradients (feels cheap, 2010s)
❌ Literal architecture (buildings, skyscrapers)
❌ Eye of providence, pyramids (conspiracy vibes)
❌ Too intricate (won't scale to avatar size)
❌ Trendy elements (will date quickly)

## Deliverables Needed

1. **Primary logo** (horizontal lockup)
2. **Avatar version** (square, simplified)
3. **Monochrome version** (black on white, white on black)
4. **Icon-only version** (for favicon, small applications)
5. **Submark** (minimal symbol for wax seals, stamps)
6. **Social media kit** (Twitter header, Substack header)

## Approval Criteria

The logo works if:
- ✓ Readable at 32x32px (favicon)
- ✓ Distinctive in black and white
- ✓ Feels expensive without being flashy
- ✓ Suggests intelligence, not just information
- ✓ Would look appropriate embossed on leather

## Budget Note

If hiring a designer:
- Expect $2,000-5,000 for full identity package
- Require 3 concept rounds
- Request all deliverables in vector format
- Specify exclusive rights (no template reuse)

If DIY:
- Use Figma (free) or Sketch
- Start with geometric shapes
- Test at multiple sizes immediately
- Get feedback from 3 people in target demographic

## Timeline

- Week 1: Concept exploration (3 directions)
- Week 2: Refinement (1 direction)
- Week 3: Finalize + deliverables
- Week 4: Implementation (Substack, Twitter, assets)

## Immediate Action

If you need a placeholder logo today:

1. Open Figma or Canva
2. Create 400x400 canvas
3. Place solid black circle as background
4. Add thin amber compass rose (from icon library)
5. Add "TA" in white, bold, center
6. Export as PNG

This buys you 2-4 weeks to develop proper identity.
