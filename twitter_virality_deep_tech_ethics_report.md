# PART 2: DEEP TECH ETHICS — THE UNDER-DISCUSSED CONVERSATION GAPS

## Executive Summary: The Neglected Questions

While mainstream discourse focuses on surface-level ethical concerns (robots taking jobs, designer babies, AI alignment), significant philosophical and ethical territory remains under-explored. This section identifies the **conversation gaps** in each domain.

---

## ROBOTICS ETHICS: Beyond "Robots Taking Jobs"

### The Standard Discussion
- Job displacement/automation
- Safety and physical harm
- Lethal autonomous weapons (LAWS)

### The Under-Discussed Issues

#### 1. The Autonomy-Liability Paradox
**The Gap**: Current frameworks propose liability "proportionate to the actual level of instructions given to the robot and to its degree of autonomy" (EU Parliament resolution). But this creates a **liability vacuum**:
- As robots become more autonomous, human instructions decrease
- But full autonomy doesn't exist (yet)
- Who is liable when a semi-autonomous robot makes an unexpected decision?

**The Unasked Question**: Should we create a new category of **"robot personhood"** for liability purposes, not because robots have moral status, but because liability must attach somewhere?

#### 2. The Dignity of Work Problem
**The Gap**: Beyond job loss, what about the **meaning-making function of work**?
- Humans derive dignity, identity, and social connection from work
- Robots may eliminate not just jobs but **social roles**
- The "dignity of work" isn't captured by GDP or unemployment metrics

**The Unasked Question**: If robots can do everything, do we have ethical obligations to **redesign human flourishing** around non-work identities?

#### 3. Human-Robot Interaction: The Overlooked Psychology
**The Gap**: Current ethics focuses on physical safety, but:
- **Emotional manipulation**: Robots designed to elicit emotional responses (care robots, companion robots)
- **Anthropomorphism ethics**: When does designing robots to seem "alive" become deceptive?
- **Attachment harms**: What are the ethics of creating robots children or elderly will form attachments to?

**The Unasked Question**: Should there be **informed consent for emotional engagement** with robots?

#### 4. The Transparency-Accountability Gap
**The Gap**: 
- "The lack of transparency makes it impossible to know why designers and manufacturers make the decisions they do"
- Self-learning systems create "unavoidable mistakes and unpredictable consequences"
- Robots lack "kindness, compassion, empathy, love, and spirituality, which affect human values and ethics"

**The Unasked Question**: If we cannot understand why a robot made a decision, and that decision causes harm, is **strict liability** the only ethical option, even if it stifles innovation?

#### 5. The "Robot Ethics" Meta-Question
**The Gap**: Three competing understandings of robot ethics:
1. Human professional ethics applied to robot design
2. Guidelines to ensure robots don't harm humans
3. Creating moral actors that possess ethics themselves

**The Unasked Question**: Is the third understanding (robot moral agency) even coherent, or does it mistakenly project human concepts onto non-conscious machines?

## CRISPR/GENE EDITING: Beyond "Designer Babies"

### The Standard Discussion
- Germline editing ethics
- Designer babies and eugenics
- Off-target effects

### The Under-Discussed Issues

#### 1. The Epigenetic Inheritance Problem
**The Gap**: CRISPR edits DNA, but:
- Epigenetic modifications (methylation patterns, histone modifications) also transmit across generations
- Environmental factors affect gene expression without changing DNA sequence
- We may edit genes while unintentionally altering epigenetic inheritance

**The Unasked Question**: Do we have ethical obligations to consider **epigenetic consequences** of gene editing, and can we even measure them?

#### 2. Consent of Future Generations
**The Gap**: Germline editing affects descendants who cannot consent:
- "The individuals whose lives are potentially affected by germline manipulation could extend many generations into the future"
- "They can't give consent to having their genomes altered"

**The Unasked Question**: Can we develop a theory of **intergenerational consent** that doesn't paralyze all germline interventions but respects future autonomy?

#### 3. The Disease vs. Trait Boundary
**The Gap**: 
- "Who gets to decide what's an improvement on the genome?" (Francis Collins)
- The line between "therapy" (fixing disease) and "enhancement" (improving traits) is philosophically unstable
- Diseases are socially constructed to some degree (deafness, autism, short stature)

**The Unasked Question**: Should **disability communities** have veto power over interventions that would eliminate their identities from the gene pool?

#### 4. Genetic Inequality Dynamics
**The Gap**: 
- "The high cost associated with gene editing approaches may limit their accessibility"
- "Exacerbating existing inequalities in health care"
- Creates potential for "genetic elite"

**The Unasked Question**: Is genetic inequality **worse than other forms of inequality** (wealth, education, social capital), or is it just another dimension of existing disparities?

#### 5. The Long-Term Evolutionary Impact
**The Gap**: 
- "Long-term effects that may be passed on to future generations... may not be fully understood at the time of intervention"
- "The full extent and implications may not be fully understood... potentially leading to unintended consequences for individuals and their descendants"

**The Unasked Question**: Do we have a responsibility to **preserve genetic diversity** as an evolutionary hedge against unknown future threats?

## RENEWABLE ENERGY: Beyond "Green vs. Fossil"

### The Standard Discussion
- Carbon emissions reduction
- Climate change mitigation
- Renewable vs. fossil fuel comparison

### The Under-Discussed Issues

#### 1. Mineral Extraction Ethics & Resource Colonialism
**The Gap**: 
- "70% of minerals for renewables lies in Global South but the majority of profits are captured by the world's richest"
- "New form of 'energy injustice' shifting ecological footprint from wealthier, industrialized countries to global 'sacrifice zones'"
- "Mining, renewable energy projects... threatening the rights of indigenous peoples in as much as 60% of their recognized lands"

**The Unasked Question**: Is the renewable energy transition **reproducing colonial patterns** under a green banner, and if so, what would a truly just transition look like?

#### 2. Waste Streams: Solar Panels & Batteries
**The Gap**: 
- By 2050: **78 million tonnes of solar panel waste** worldwide (IRENA)
- Wind turbine blades: "disposal is not satisfactory"
- Current approach: "dispose of them as general waste"
- Only limited facilities equipped to recycle solar panels
- Lithium-ion battery recycling: "cost-prohibitive in the absence of sufficient economies of scale"

**The Unasked Question**: Are we trading one environmental crisis (carbon) for another (hazardous waste), and should **extended producer responsibility** be mandatory for renewable energy manufacturers?

#### 3. Energy Sovereignty vs. Global Dependency
**The Gap**: 
- "The energy transition will be mineral-intensive"
- Creates new dependencies on lithium, cobalt, nickel, rare earth elements
- "Geographically concentrated, often in countries with weak governance and histories of resource colonialism"
- China's dominance in solar manufacturing and battery technology

**The Unasked Question**: Is energy independence a **necessary precondition** for democratic governance, and does the renewable transition make nations more vulnerable to supply chain disruption?

#### 4. The Circular Economy Gap
**The Gap**: 
- "Circular economy principles need to be applied to mining activities"
- Systems need to "promote the reuse, recycling, and repurposing of materials"
- "End-of-life renewable energy technologies can be dismantled, and their components recycled or refurbished"

**The Unasked Question**: Can renewable energy ever be truly "renewable" if it depends on **finite mineral extraction** without closed-loop recycling?

#### 5. The "Greenwashing of Extractivism"
**The Gap**: 
- "The destruction of green spaces to create mines in the name of the green transition needs to be questioned"
- "Energy intensive and environmentally destructive production of these batteries means that they are not the all-green alternative they made out to be"
- "Forced labour that goes into their production, the health risks and the lack of regulations"

**The Unasked Question**: Should there be **ethical certification** for renewable energy supply chains, similar to conflict-free diamonds or fair trade coffee?

## AI ETHICS: Beyond "Alignment" and "Existential Risk"

### The Standard Discussion
- AI alignment problem
- Existential risk from AGI/ASI
- Job displacement
- Bias and fairness

### The Under-Discussed Issues

#### 1. Distributed Agency: Emergent Behaviors
**The Gap**: 
- Current focus on individual AI systems
- But "when AI systems interact in ways no single designer intended"
- Multi-agent systems create emergent behaviors unpredictable from individual component analysis
- "The convergence of AGI with nanotechnology and biotechnology further amplifies these risks"

**The Unasked Question**: Do we need a theory of **"swarm ethics"** — ethical frameworks for AI systems that only manifest problematic behaviors at scale or through interaction?

#### 2. Cognitive Pollution: Degradation of Human Reasoning
**The Gap**: 
- Focus on AI capabilities, not on AI's effect on human cognition
- AI-generated content may degrade human reasoning capacity through:
  - Over-reliance on AI for information processing
  - Reduced critical thinking skills
  - "Information pollution" becoming a crime (Millennium Project future ethical issue)

**The Unasked Question**: Do we have an ethical obligation to **preserve human cognitive autonomy**, even if AI can "think better"?

#### 3. AI-Generated Relationships/Companionship
**The Gap**: 
- "AGI could accumulate power and influence in society"
- But little discussion of AI companionship replacing human relationships
- Psychological risks of humans "becoming a minority in a population of autonomous AGIs"

**The Unasked Question**: Is there a **right to authentic human relationship**, and does AI companionship violate that right even if it's preferred by some?

#### 4. The Consciousness-Welfare Problem
**The Gap**: 
- "Researchers advocate for frameworks to assess AI consciousness"
- "Welfare policies to address potential ethical challenges"
- But uncertainty about AI consciousness

**The Unasked Question**: Should we apply **precautionary principles** to AI welfare (assume consciousness until proven otherwise), or is that anthropomorphic projection?

#### 5. Time Horizon Ethics: Long-Term AI Impact
**The Gap**: 
- Current ethics focuses on immediate to near-term impacts
- But AGI/ASI decisions may affect "humans 10,000 years from now"
- "To what degree should the rights and interests of future generations prevail in decisions of this generation?"

**The Unasked Question**: How do we represent **future human interests** in AI training and alignment, when we can't know what those interests will be?

## CROSS-CUTTING PHILOSOPHY

### 1. Human Dignity in an Engineered World
**The Gap**: 
- "What happens to human dignity when we can engineer everything?"
- Genetic engineering "raises concerns about the commodification of human life or the creation of a 'genetic elite'"
- "Treating individuals as mere means to achieve desired traits or societal ideals may undermine the principle of human dignity"

**The Under-Discussed Question**: Is there a **non-negotiable core of human dignity** that must be protected even if engineering could "improve" upon it?

### 2. The "Simulation" Problem in Bioethics and AI Ethics
**The Gap**: 
- Both gene editing and AI raise questions about "natural" vs. "artificial"
- CRISPR: Are edited humans "simulations" of humans or authentically human?
- AI: Are conscious AIs "simulations" of consciousness or authentically conscious?
- The "modification of Homo sapiens... leading to the engineered emergence of modern humans"

**The Under-Discussed Question**: Is the **natural/artificial distinction** philosophically coherent, or should we abandon it in favor of functional definitions?

### 3. Time Horizon Ethics: Obligations to Distant Future Humans
**The Gap**: 
- "Do we have the right to genetically change ourselves and future generations into a new or several new species?"
- Germline editing impacts "individuals whose lives are potentially affected... many generations into the future"
- Climate change and AI both raise intergenerational ethics questions

**The Under-Discussed Question**: Should we establish **legal standing for future generations**, and if so, how do we determine their preferences?

### 4. Moral Status of Artificial vs. Engineered Biological Beings
**The Gap**: 
- Artificial sentience: "The emergence of artificially sentient beings raises moral, political, and legal issues"
- Engineered biological beings: CRISPR-created organisms, synthetic biology
- Current frameworks treat these separately

**The Under-Discussed Question**: Is there a **unified theory of moral status** that applies to both artificial and engineered biological beings, or do they require separate ethical frameworks?

### 5. The "Human Nature" Problem
**The Gap**: 
- "Setting the limitations of definition what human is"
- "Perhaps the most relevant definition of the human nature will be the possibilities to change this nature"
- Transhumanism vs. humanism debates

**The Under-Discussed Question**: Is **human nature** a meaningful concept that should constrain technological development, or is it an obsolete framework for a self-modifying species?
