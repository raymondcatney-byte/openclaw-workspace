## USAGE NOTES

**When to use which:**

| Template | Best For |
|----------|----------|
| **Template 1** | General applications, cold outreach, when you don't know the specific role yet |
| **Template 2** | AI/ML roles at biotech companies, computational biology positions |
| **Template 3** | Energy trading desks, blockchain RWA protocols, carbon credit platforms |
| **Template 4** | Quant funds, crypto hedge funds, multi-asset strategies |
| **Template 5** | Seed/Series A startups, founding engineer roles, generalist positions |

**Customization tips:**

1. **Always** research the company and add 1-2 sentences showing you understand their specific problem
2. **If they have a technical blog:** Reference a specific post and connect it to your work
3. **If they're hiring for a specific project:** Lead with the relevant domain section (e.g., lead with biotech for Recursion, energy for Crusoe)
4. **If you're applying through a warm intro:** Remove the "skepticism" paragraphs—they're for cold outreach credibility establishment

**Follow-up strategy:**

- **Day 3:** If no response, send GitHub repo link with one-line: "Production code from my A2A server: [link]"
- **Day 7:** If no response, send relevant Substack post: "Wrote about [their domain] last month. Thought you might find this interesting: [link]"
- **Day 14:** Final follow-up: "I know you're busy. If the timing's wrong, just reply 'not now' and I'll check back in 3 months."

**Tone calibration:**

These are confident—some might say arrogant. That's intentional. You're compensating for lack of credentials with strong positioning. But if the company culture seems more humble/academic, dial back the "I know before we meet" language and emphasize curiosity/learning instead.
